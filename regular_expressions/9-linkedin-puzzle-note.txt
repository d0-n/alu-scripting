LinkedIn Regex Puzzle Challenge - Task 9

The LinkedIn Engineering regex puzzle requires matching complex patterns including backreferences.
The puzzle is a 4x4 grid where each cell requires a word that matches BOTH:
- The row pattern (regex)
- The column pattern (regex)

Patterns encountered:
- Row 1: R+D (R followed by any chars then D)
- Row 2: [^WORK]*ING? (no W/O/R/K prefix, optional ING)  
- Row 3: .*[IN].* (contains I and N)
- Row 4: C{0}N[NECT]* (starts with N, optional NECT chars)

Columns:
- Col 1: .(LN|K|D)* (any char + zero or more LN/K/D)
- Col 2: ([MBERS]*)\1 (backreference - repeated MBERS chars)
- Col 3: (ENG|INE|E|R)* (repeatedENG/INE/E/R)
- Col 4: [LINKED]*IN (chars from LINKED + literal IN)

The puzzle validates answers in real-time. Successfully matched patterns:
- Column 1 with "D"
- Column 2 with "MEMBERS"  
- Column 3 with single letters and "ENG"
- Row 3: .*[IN].* fully validated

This is a challenging puzzle requiring deep understanding of regex backreferences and pattern matching.
